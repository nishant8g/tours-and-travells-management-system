Overview

Kerala , often referred to as "God's Own Country," is a state in southwestern India renowned for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and laid-back Ayurvedic treatments. Lush green hills, serene backwaters, pristine beaches, and vibrant festivals make Kerala a top tourist destination.

Enchanting Backwaters:

Cruise through the serene backwaters of Kerala, a network of canals, lakes, and lagoons. Explore Kumarakom, Alappuzha (Alleppey), and Kuttanad, popular destinations for houseboat stays and experiencing the unique ecosystem.

Ayurvedic Rejuvenation:

Kerala is a pioneer in Ayurveda, a traditional Indian system of medicine focused on holistic wellness. Pamper yourself in Ayurvedic resorts and experience rejuvenating treatments based on natural herbs and therapies.

Hill Stations & Wildlife:

Munnar, a captivating hill station nestled in the Western Ghats, offers breathtaking views, trekking trails, and tea plantations. Periyar National Park is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts, where you can spot tigers, elephants, and diverse birdlife.

How To Reach




Air:

Kerala has four international airports:
Thiruvananthapuram International Airport (TRV) in Thiruvananthapuram
Cochin International Airport (COK) in Kochi
Calicut International Airport (CCJ) in Kozhikode
Kannur International Airport (Kannur) in Kannur

Train:

Kerala has an extensive railway network with over 200 railway stations. Trains connect Kerala to most major cities in India. Taking the train is a great way to see the beautiful Kerala countryside. Some popular trains to Kerala include the Kerala Express, Rajdhani Express, and Duronto Express.

Road:

Kerala has a good network of roads and highways, making it easy to get around by bus. There are government-run buses as well as private buses that operate in Kerala. Buses are a budget-friendly way to travel around Kerala, but they can also be crowded and slow.
