Overview

The Andaman and Nicobar Islands , a union territory of India, is an archipelago offering contrasting experiences on two distinct island groups

Unveiling Paradise:

Andaman boasts some of India's most stunning beaches like Radhanagar Beach, Havelock Island Beach, and Elephant Beach, perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.
Emerald Forests: Dense rainforests teeming with exotic flora and fauna cover a significant portion of the islands. Explore these lush landscapes through trekking trails, encounter vibrant birdlife, or spot indigenous wildlife like wild elephants and Andaman macaques.

Adventure Playground:

Scuba Diving & Snorkelling: Andaman's crystal-clear waters and vibrant coral reefs make it a haven for scuba diving and snorkeling enthusiasts. Explore the underwater world teeming with colorful marine life, from playful dolphins to majestic manta rays.
Water Sports: Get your adrenaline pumping with various water sports activities like jet skiing, parasailing, and kayaking. Enjoy thrilling rides on the waves or explore hidden coves and mangrove forests from a unique perspective.

Cultural Tapestry:

Cellular Jail: A poignant reminder of India's colonial past, the Cellular Jail in Port Blair served as a cellular jail for Indian freedom fighters. Explore its architecture and learn about the struggles for independence.

How To Reach




Air:

Veer Savarkar International Airport (PSO) at Port Blair is the sole airport in Andaman and Nicobar Islands.
Direct domestic flights are available from various major Indian cities, including Kolkata, Chennai, Delhi, Bangalore, Hyderabad, and Bhubaneswar.

Ship:

Government ferries operate regularly from mainland India to Port Blair. The journey takes approximately 50-60 hours, depending on the departing port. Major ports with ferry services to Port Blair include:
Chennai
Kolkata
Visakhapatnam
